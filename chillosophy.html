  • ai would be capable of discovering things in ways that humans would never think of because, well, they aren't human. yet, it's our challenge to understand and use the ideas that they create -- math proofs, art, science
  • Back then, people thought the pursuit of philosophy was the pursuit of some hidden knowledge that would allow them to make sense of the world and their reality. They created “grand narratives,” stories and ideas that try to organize why everything is the way it is. The grand narrative of Christianity is that the resurrection of Jesus will allow him to save humans from their sins. Even non-religious beliefs like logic and its basic principles such as cause and effect try to provide a universal basis for understanding everything (even though it doesn’t seem like it). But there are so many unknowns and incomplete areas in any of these stories, leading to the idea that philosophy isn’t about constructing these new theories about the world but breaking down systems of power and norms that were previously upheld. This is widely considered the ultimate purpose of philosophy.
